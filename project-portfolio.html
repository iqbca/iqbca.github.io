AI-Powered Portfolio Analytics Platform

This case study documents the design and ownership of an institutional-grade portfolio analytics platform built to deliver consistent performance, attribution, risk, and diagnostic analytics across portfolios and reporting cycles.

The primary objective was not to increase analytical sophistication, but to replace fragmented, spreadsheet-heavy workflows with a governed, scalable analytics system.

Context & Problem

Portfolio analytics were historically produced through a combination of spreadsheets, manual adjustments, and analyst-specific logic. While workable at small scale, this approach introduced significant challenges as portfolio coverage expanded.

Key issues included inconsistent analytics across portfolios, limited reusability of logic, difficulty validating outputs, and heavy dependency on individual analysts to interpret results.

Design Objectives

Consistency

Ensure identical analytics logic and outputs across portfolios, managers, and reporting periods.

Scalability

Enable onboarding of new portfolios without exponential increases in manual effort or complexity.

Governance

Separate data, assumptions, analytics logic, and presentation layers to improve control and auditability.

Solution Architecture

The platform was designed as a modular analytics engine using Python for computation and Power BI for presentation.

Data ingestion, analytics computation, and visualisation were deliberately separated, allowing changes in one layer without breaking downstream outputs.

This design enabled analytics logic to be reused across portfolios and reporting cycles while maintaining consistent outputs.

Analytics Coverage

Performance Analytics

Absolute and relative returns across periods, including cumulative and periodic performance views.

Attribution Analysis

Multi-level attribution across asset classes and managers, enabling clear performance explanation.

Risk & Diagnostics

Volatility, drawdowns, downside metrics, and diagnostic checks to support risk-aware decision-making.

Rolling Analysis

Rolling returns and consistency metrics to assess behaviour across market cycles.

Impact & Outcomes

The platform significantly reduced manual effort per reporting cycle, improved consistency across portfolios, and enabled analytics reuse across clients and time periods.

Most importantly, it shifted analytics from being analyst-dependent outputs to infrastructure-level capability.

Key Takeaways

The value of the platform was not increased complexity, but improved trust, repeatability, and scalability. Analytics became a system that could be reviewed, validated, and evolved without breaking.

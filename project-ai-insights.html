AI Portfolio Insights Engine

This case study documents the design of an AI-assisted portfolio insights engine created to convert structured analytics outputs into consistent, explainable investment commentary.

The objective was not to automate judgement, but to reduce repetitive interpretation work while preserving analyst oversight and accountability.

Context & Problem

Portfolio analytics outputs were accurate but required manual interpretation and narrative writing for every reporting cycle.

This led to inconsistent commentary styles, variable depth of explanation, and significant time spent on repetitive drafting tasks.

Design Principles

Explainability

Every narrative statement must be traceable to underlying metrics and data.

Constraints

AI outputs are restricted by predefined rules, templates, and thresholds.

Human Oversight

Analysts review, edit, and approve all generated commentary before delivery.

System Design

The engine operates on structured analytics inputs such as performance, attribution, risk metrics, and rolling analysis outputs.

These inputs are mapped to narrative templates and logic layers that guide AI-generated text, ensuring relevance and consistency.

The AI layer is deliberately constrained to interpretation tasks and does not modify or generate analytics.

Outputs & Use Cases

Portfolio Commentary

Structured explanations of performance drivers, risk changes, and attribution effects.

Change Detection

Highlighting periods or metrics requiring analyst attention.

Consistency

Standardised narrative tone and structure across portfolios and reporting cycles.

Impact & Outcomes

The insights engine reduced time spent on narrative drafting, improved consistency of commentary, and allowed analysts to focus on review and interpretation.

It also created a foundation for integrating AI safely into analytics workflows without undermining trust.

Key Takeaways

AI adds the most value when used to narrow attention and reduce friction — not when it attempts to replace analytical judgement.

How I Think About Analytics

Over time, my work has shifted from producing individual analyses to designing systems that can deliver analytics reliably, repeatedly, and with minimal key-person risk.

I believe strong investment analytics is not defined by complex models alone, but by clear structure, validated data flows, controlled assumptions, and outputs that decision-makers can trust.

This perspective shapes how I approach automation, reporting design, and the integration of tools such as Python, Excel, and Power BI into cohesive analytics frameworks.
